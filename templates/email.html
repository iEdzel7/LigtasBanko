    What is PwN?
    "PwN" is a slang term meaning that a person’s data has been compromised or stolen in a breach. It indicates that sensitive information, like email addresses or passwords, been exposed or taken by unauthorized parties, often through hacking.
    What will I do?
    If your data has been "pwned," it's important to take action quickly. hange your affected passwords immediately, especially for sensitive accounts like email, banking, and social media.

    Use strong, unique passwords for each account and consider enabling two-factor authentication (2FA) for added security.

    Additionally, monitor your accounts for any suspicious activity and alert relevant service providers if necessary.
    Change your password
    Regularly update your passwords to make it harder for hackers to access your accounts. Use strong, unique passwords that combine uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
    Change password
    Enable 2FA
    Activate two-factor authentication (2FA) for an extra layer of security. This requires a secondary code or device to verify your identity, protecting your account even if your password is compromised.
